Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert of the MIT AI Lab, released a extremely critical critique called Perceptrons: An Introduction to Computational Geometry

- Minsky & Papert (1969, pp. 231-232)

1986:

The Backpropogation Paradigm

Backpropgation Logic

  • Compute the output with an activation function that was non-linear & differentiable (building on Adaline)
  • Use derivative and adjust weights to minimize error
  • Apply chain rule to compute derivatives for all neurons in a prior layer and use to adjust their weights as well (i.e. backpropogate the error)

Medical Image Interpretation

Scenario: Medical image classification (with ultrasounds, CAT scans, X-rays, etc.) plays a key role in disease detection and early treatment for a wide range of ailments

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)Â provide an ideal framework for image classification (Li et al. 2014, Anwar et al. 2018, Yadav & Jadhav 2019):

  1. Convolutional layers focus on isolated portions of image
    • Weights are shared across entire image, reducing parameters
  2. Pooling layers reduce spatial size
  3. Consecutive convolutional-pooling rounds allow hierarchical feature learning
    • Early layers learn simple edges/lines, later layers capture complex structures

Scenario: A large corpus of unstructured qualitative and quantitative health data exists (electronic/paper health records, social media posts, etc.) that can be mined and analyzed for insights on pop. health

Literature shows a wide range of NN architectures can be used for NLP (Zhang et al. 2017, Conway et al. 2019).
